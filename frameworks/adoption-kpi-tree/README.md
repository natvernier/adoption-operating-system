# Adoption KPI Tree

A framework for translating AI and digital adoption ambitions into measurable signals, operating decisions and improvement actions.

## What this is

The Adoption KPI Tree is a measurement and decision framework for organizations that want adoption to become visible, discussable and actionable.

It connects strategic ambition with everyday workflow behavior, value creation, quality, risk and operating decisions.

The central question is:

```text
What would we need to see in the work system to know that adoption is actually happening?
```

## Why it matters

AI and digital adoption often fail in the space between strategic ambition and everyday work.

Organizations may provide tools, licenses, trainings, communications or pilots. These activities can support adoption, but they do not prove that work has changed.

A strong adoption measurement model does not stop at activity.

It asks whether:

* relevant workflows are changing
* people are using new capabilities in meaningful situations
* repeat behavior is emerging
* quality remains reliable or improves
* risk is handled earlier and more visibly
* human judgment points are clear
* successful patterns become reusable
* operating decisions improve because signals are visible

The Adoption KPI Tree helps organizations move from vague transformation language to measurable adoption logic.

## Core principle

An Adoption KPI Tree is not a reporting structure.

It is a decision structure.

The value of an adoption KPI is not the number itself. The value is the action the organization is willing to take when the signal changes.

```text
If a KPI does not support a decision,
it may be reporting noise.
```

## Basic structure

An Adoption KPI Tree usually connects five levels:

```text
1. Strategic adoption ambition
2. Adoption outcomes
3. Behavioral and workflow signals
4. Quality, value and risk indicators
5. Operating actions
```

This structure helps teams connect strategy, behavior, evidence and action.

## When to use this framework

Use the Adoption KPI Tree when an organization needs to understand whether AI or digital adoption is becoming part of everyday work.

It can support:

* AI adoption strategies
* transformation programs
* product or platform rollouts
* internal enablement initiatives
* governance and review routines
* leadership discussions
* adoption retrospectives
* pilot evaluations
* scaling decisions
* workflow redesign
* adoption portfolio reviews

## What this framework is not

This is not a universal KPI catalogue.

It is not a dashboard template.

It is not a replacement for analytics, user research, qualitative feedback, governance review or operational judgment.

It is a structuring framework for deciding which adoption signals matter, why they matter and what should happen when they change.
