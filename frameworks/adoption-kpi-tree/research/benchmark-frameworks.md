# Benchmark Frameworks

This document compares the Adoption KPI Tree with established research traditions, professional frameworks and management models.

The purpose is not to claim that the Adoption KPI Tree replaces these frameworks.

The purpose is to show what each benchmark framework contributes and where the Adoption KPI Tree extends the logic for operational adoption measurement.

## Evaluation lens

Each benchmark framework is reviewed through five questions:

```text
What is it useful for?
What does it measure or explain well?
What does it not fully solve?
What does the Adoption KPI Tree take from it?
What does the Adoption KPI Tree add?
```

## 1. Prosci ADKAR and change ROI metrics

## What it is useful for

Prosci ADKAR is useful for understanding individual change readiness and progression.

It focuses on whether people move through stages such as awareness, desire, knowledge, ability and reinforcement.

Prosci also connects change outcomes to speed of adoption, ultimate utilization and proficiency.

## What it measures or explains well

It explains:

* whether people understand the change
* whether people are willing to participate
* whether people have the knowledge to change
* whether people can perform in the new way
* whether the change is reinforced
* how speed, utilization and proficiency influence project value

## What it does not fully solve

It does not fully solve:

* workflow-level signal design
* AI governance signals
* quality and risk countermetrics
* operational decision thresholds
* product-style adoption analytics
* detailed if-signal-then-action logic

## What the Adoption KPI Tree takes from it

The Adoption KPI Tree takes:

* adoption speed
* utilization
* proficiency
* role-based barriers
* reinforcement logic
* manager responsibility
* the distinction between communication and real adoption

## What the Adoption KPI Tree adds

The Adoption KPI Tree adds:

* workflow signals
* quality signals
* risk signals
* governance signals
* scaling signals
* countermetrics
* decision thresholds
* operating actions

## 2. Technology Acceptance Model and UTAUT

## What it is useful for

Technology acceptance research is useful for understanding why people accept or reject technologies.

The Technology Acceptance Model emphasizes perceived usefulness and perceived ease of use.

UTAUT extends acceptance research with factors such as social influence and facilitating conditions.

## What it measures or explains well

It explains:

* perceived usefulness
* perceived ease of use
* behavioral intention
* actual use
* social influence
* facilitating conditions
* acceptance barriers

## What it does not fully solve

It does not fully solve:

* whether use changes work
* whether outputs improve
* whether value is created
* whether risk is governed
* whether adoption is scalable
* whether signals trigger operating decisions

## What the Adoption KPI Tree takes from it

The Adoption KPI Tree takes:

* usefulness as a trust and value signal
* ease of use as an activation and workflow signal
* social influence as a role and manager signal
* facilitating conditions as enablement and governance context
* the distinction between intention and actual use

## What the Adoption KPI Tree adds

The Adoption KPI Tree adds:

* first meaningful use
* repeat workflow behavior
* workflow integration
* quality and risk interpretation
* governance signals
* decision logic beyond acceptance

## 4. DORA metrics

## What it is useful for

DORA is useful for measuring software delivery performance.

It is especially valuable because it balances speed and stability instead of treating output volume as success.

## What it measures or explains well

DORA measures:

* deployment frequency
* lead time for changes
* change failure rate
* failed deployment recovery time
* reliability

## What it does not fully solve

It does not fully solve:

* non-engineering adoption contexts
* AI adoption in knowledge work
* human review workflows
* trust signals
* role-based adoption
* governance and risk interpretation outside software delivery

## What the Adoption KPI Tree takes from it

The Adoption KPI Tree takes:

* speed must be balanced with stability
* delivery performance needs countermetrics
* throughput alone is not enough
* operational performance must be linked to outcomes

## What the Adoption KPI Tree adds

The Adoption KPI Tree generalizes the speed-versus-stability logic.

For adoption, this means:

```text
Usage must be balanced with quality.
Speed must be balanced with review effort.
Value must be balanced with risk.
Scaling must be balanced with trust and governance.
```

## What it does not fully solve

It does not fully solve:

* adoption-specific signal categories

## What the Adoption KPI Tree takes from it

## 6. NIST AI Risk Management Framework

## What it is useful for

The NIST AI Risk Management Framework is useful for structuring AI risk management.

It organizes AI risk management activities around functions such as Govern, Map, Measure and Manage.

## What it measures or explains well

It helps organizations think about:

* AI risk identification
* governance structures
* mapping context
* measuring risk
* managing risk
* accountability
* trustworthy AI characteristics
* lifecycle risk management

## What it does not fully solve

It does not fully solve:

* adoption signal design
* workflow-specific adoption measurement
* day-to-day interpretation of adoption signals
* role-based adoption behavior
* operating rituals
* practical if-signal-then-action logic

## What the Adoption KPI Tree takes from it

The Adoption KPI Tree takes:

* risk must be governed
* context matters
* risk should be mapped and measured
* governance should be continuous
* AI adoption needs accountability and oversight

## What the Adoption KPI Tree adds

The Adoption KPI Tree operationalizes governance through signals such as:

* review gates completed
* escalation path usage
* repeated policy confusion
* unclear output ownership
* sensitive use cases without escalation
* quality gates bypassed
* distinction between experiment, draft, reviewed output and approved output

## Final principle

The Adoption KPI Tree is not a replacement for established frameworks.

It is an operating translation layer.

It helps organizations move from strategy, principles and adoption ambition to observable work-system signals and decisions.
