# University and Expert Resources

This document maps university programs, research centers, expert institutions and professional bodies that are relevant to the Adoption KPI Tree.

The goal is not to claim that top-tier universities teach “Adoption KPI Trees” as a named discipline. They usually do not.

Instead, they teach the surrounding disciplines:

* AI strategy
* digital transformation
* responsible AI
* organizational change
* technology adoption
* work redesign
* product and UX measurement
* governance and risk management
* leadership for AI-enabled organizations

The Adoption KPI Tree translates these disciplines into an operating framework for adoption measurement.

## Purpose

Use this file to understand where the Adoption KPI Tree connects to established research, executive education and expert practice.

The research layer helps answer:

```text
Which credible institutions already study the themes behind adoption measurement?
```

And:

```text
Where does the Adoption KPI Tree add practical operating structure?
```

## 6. DORA, Google Cloud and software delivery research

## Why it is relevant

DORA is relevant because it is a strong benchmark for balanced operational measurement.

It does not treat speed alone as success.

It balances speed, stability, reliability and organizational performance.

## Useful themes

* delivery speed
* stability
* reliability
* throughput
* failure rate
* recovery
* countermetrics
* organizational performance
* high-performing teams

## Relevance for the Adoption KPI Tree

DORA is useful as an analogy for adoption measurement.

Just as software delivery should not be measured only by speed, adoption should not be measured only by usage.
