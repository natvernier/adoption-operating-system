# Research Map

This document maps the research fields, professional frameworks and expert traditions that inform the Adoption KPI Tree.

The Adoption KPI Tree is not based on one discipline alone.

It sits at the intersection of change management, technology acceptance, product metrics, UX measurement, software delivery performance, AI governance, enterprise transformation and organizational learning.

## Purpose

The purpose of this research map is to show which established bodies of knowledge the Adoption KPI Tree builds on and where it adds operational precision.

The framework does not try to replace these disciplines.

It translates their relevant ideas into a practical adoption measurement structure for workflows, roles, value, risk and operating decisions.

## Core thesis

Most adoption measurement systems over-measure activity and under-measure operating change.

They often track:

* tool access
* training completion
* communication reach
* login counts
* active users
* pilot count
* number of generated outputs

These signals can be useful.

But they do not prove that adoption has changed work, improved quality, created value, clarified accountability or reduced unmanaged risk.

The Adoption KPI Tree asks a different question:

```text
What would we need to see in the work system to know that adoption is actually happening?
```

## Positioning of the Adoption KPI Tree

The Adoption KPI Tree is a practical synthesis.

It is informed by established frameworks, but it is designed for operational use.

It helps teams move from abstract adoption ambition to observable signals and action.

## What the framework adds

The Adoption KPI Tree adds five things that are often missing from adoption measurement:

## 1. Signal categories

It separates different types of adoption evidence:

* usage
* activation
* workflow
* capability
* quality
* value
* risk
* trust
* governance
* scaling

## 2. Workflow focus

It treats adoption as a change in the work system, not only as tool usage.

## 3. Value and risk balance

It connects adoption to benefit while also making risk, trust and governance visible.

## 4. Interpretation logic

It avoids false conclusions by asking what a signal may mean in context.

## 5. Operating action

It connects signals to decisions:

```text
If [signal],
then [interpretation],
therefore [action].
```

## Research positioning statement

The Adoption KPI Tree is a practical framework for making adoption observable, interpretable and actionable.

It synthesizes ideas from change management, technology acceptance, product analytics, UX measurement, software delivery performance, AI governance, enterprise transformation and organizational learning.

Its distinctive contribution is the translation of these ideas into a workflow-based operating structure:

```text
Strategic ambition
→ adoption outcomes
→ behavioral and workflow signals
→ quality, value and risk indicators
→ decision logic
→ operating action
```

## Final principle

Adoption measurement should not only prove that activity happened.

It should help the organization understand how work is changing, where value is emerging, where risk is becoming visible and what should happen next.
