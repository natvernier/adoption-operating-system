# Reading List

This reading list collects books, frameworks, papers, standards, research centers, newsletters and expert sources that are relevant to the Adoption KPI Tree.

It is designed as a practical learning path, not as a complete academic bibliography.

## Purpose

Use this reading list to understand the fields behind adoption measurement:

* change management
* technology acceptance
* product and UX metrics
* software delivery and productivity measurement
* AI governance
* enterprise AI transformation
* organizational learning
* psychological safety
* responsible scaling

## 1. Google HEART framework

Best for:

* goals-to-signals-to-metrics logic
* product-style measurement
* adoption, retention and task success
* user-centered metric design

Why it matters:

The Adoption KPI Tree also starts with goals and expected behavior, not with available data alone.

## 2. Prosci ADKAR and change ROI material

Best for:

* individual change
* adoption speed
* ultimate utilization
* proficiency
* change barriers
* reinforcement

Why it matters:

The Adoption KPI Tree uses similar adoption logic, but extends it into workflow, governance, risk and decision action.

## 3. DORA metrics and Accelerate research

Best for:

* speed and stability
* operational performance
* delivery performance
* countermetrics
* high-performing teams

Why it matters:

DORA is a strong model for balancing speed with reliability.

The Adoption KPI Tree applies the same balancing logic to adoption.

## 4. SPACE framework

Best for:

* multi-dimensional productivity
* avoiding one-dimensional measurement
* balancing activity with performance, collaboration, satisfaction and flow

Why it matters:

SPACE supports the idea that adoption is not one metric.

A mature adoption measurement system needs multiple signal categories.

## 5. NIST AI Risk Management Framework

Best for:

* AI risk management
* lifecycle governance
* Govern, Map, Measure, Manage
* trustworthy AI
* accountability

Why it matters:

NIST supports the risk and governance branches of the Adoption KPI Tree.

## 6. ISO/IEC 42001

Best for:

* AI management systems
* responsible AI governance
* organizational accountability
* continual improvement
* risk and opportunity management

Why it matters:

ISO/IEC 42001 provides management-system logic for AI.

The Adoption KPI Tree can translate this into adoption evidence.

## 7. OECD AI Principles

Best for:

* trustworthy AI
* human-centered values
* accountability
* robustness
* safety
* responsible stewardship

Why it matters:

The OECD AI Principles support trust, accountability and governance signals.

## Core books

## Diffusion of Innovations

Author: Everett M. Rogers

Best for:

* how innovations spread
* adopter categories
* diffusion dynamics
* social systems
* communication channels
* adoption over time

Relevance:

Useful for understanding why adoption spreads unevenly across roles, teams and contexts.

## Leading Digital

Authors: George Westerman, Didier Bonnet, Andrew McAfee

Best for:

* digital transformation
* leadership
* digital maturity
* technology-enabled change
* organizational capability

Relevance:

Useful for the strategic and leadership context behind adoption.

## Designed for Digital

Authors: Jeanne W. Ross, Cynthia M. Beath, Martin Mocker

Best for:

* digital operating models
* enterprise architecture
* organizational capabilities
* digital business design
* operational foundations

Relevance:

Useful for understanding why adoption depends on operating model design, not tools alone.

## Accelerate

Authors: Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble, Gene Kim

Best for:

* DevOps research
* software delivery performance
* speed and stability
* high-performing technology organizations
* evidence-based metrics

Relevance:

Useful for countermetric thinking and operational measurement.

## The Fearless Organization

Author: Amy C. Edmondson

Best for:

* psychological safety
* speaking up
* learning behavior
* escalation culture
* team learning

Relevance:

Useful for trust signals, escalation behavior and risk visibility.

## Measure What Matters

Author: John Doerr

Best for:

* objectives and key results
* alignment
* focus
* measurable ambition

Relevance:

Useful for outcome orientation, but should be complemented with signal interpretation and countermetrics.

## Co-Intelligence

Author: Ethan Mollick

Best for:

* practical AI use
* human-AI collaboration
* AI as coworker
* experimentation with AI
* individual and team use patterns

Relevance:

Useful for understanding how people actually use AI in work and where judgment remains important.
