# Case Study Reference Map

This document maps case studies, reference examples and applied frameworks that are useful for studying adoption measurement, workflow change, AI governance and scaling.

The purpose is not to provide full case analyses.

The purpose is to identify cases that can inform Adoption KPI Tree design.

## How to use this file

Use this reference map when looking for examples that illustrate:

* adoption beyond tool access
* workflow integration
* role-based adoption
* responsible AI governance
* pilot-to-scale transition
* adoption measurement
* product-style metrics
* quality and risk countermetrics
* operating model change

Each case can be analyzed through the Adoption KPI Tree lens:

```text
What was the adoption ambition?
Which roles were affected?
Which workflows changed?
Which signals showed adoption?
Which quality, value or risk indicators mattered?
Which governance signals were visible?
Which scaling conditions were required?
```

## 1. Google HEART framework

## Why it is relevant

Google HEART is a strong reference for user-centered product measurement.

It is relevant because it shows how goals can be translated into signals and metrics.

## Adoption theme

Product and UX adoption measurement.

## Signals to study

* adoption
* retention
* engagement
* task success
* satisfaction or happiness
* goals-to-signals-to-metrics mapping

## Relevance for the Adoption KPI Tree

HEART is useful for the signal-design logic of the Adoption KPI Tree.

It shows that metrics should not start with available data alone.

They should start with goals and then identify signals.

## Adoption KPI Tree extension

The Adoption KPI Tree extends HEART by adding:

* workflow signals
* role-based signals
* quality signals
* risk signals
* governance signals
* if-signal-then-action logic

## 2. DORA research program

## Why it is relevant

DORA is a strong reference for measuring operational performance through balanced metrics.

It is relevant because it does not measure speed alone.

It balances delivery speed with stability and reliability.

## Adoption theme

Operational performance measurement and countermetrics.

## Signals to study

* deployment frequency
* lead time
* change failure rate
* recovery time
* reliability
* organizational performance
* team well-being

## Relevance for the Adoption KPI Tree

DORA is useful because adoption measurement also needs balance.

For example:

```text
More usage is not enough.
More usage must be balanced with quality, risk, trust and value.
```

## Adoption KPI Tree extension

The Adoption KPI Tree translates DORA-style balance into adoption contexts:

* usage versus quality
* speed versus review effort
* value versus risk
* scaling versus trust
* standardization versus contextual fit

## 3. SPACE framework

## Why it is relevant

SPACE is relevant because it argues that productivity cannot be measured through a single dimension.

It offers a useful analogy for adoption measurement.

Adoption also cannot be measured through one dimension.

## Adoption theme

Multi-dimensional productivity and work-system measurement.

## Signals to study

* satisfaction and well-being
* performance
* activity
* communication and collaboration
* efficiency and flow

## Relevance for the Adoption KPI Tree

SPACE supports the core Adoption KPI Tree argument:

```text
Activity is not enough.
A mature measurement system needs multiple signal categories.
```

## Adoption KPI Tree extension

The Adoption KPI Tree applies this multi-dimensional logic to adoption:

* usage
* activation
* workflow
* capability
* quality
* value
* risk
* trust
* governance
* scaling

## 4. Microsoft Copilot enterprise deployment

## Why it is relevant

Enterprise Copilot deployment is highly relevant because it represents a real-world AI adoption challenge across roles, workflows and governance contexts.

Copilot adoption is not only a question of access.

It requires role clarity, workflow integration, enablement, trust, data awareness, review routines and measurable value.

## Adoption theme

Enterprise AI adoption and role-based workflow change.

## Signals to study

* licensed users versus active users
* first meaningful use
* repeat use by role
* workflow-specific use cases
* manager support
* perceived productivity
* trust and confidence
* quality of outputs
* governance questions
* responsible use guidelines
* scaling across functions

## Relevance for the Adoption KPI Tree

This type of case is useful for studying the gap between:

```text
AI tool available
```

and:

```text
AI capability integrated into meaningful work.
```

## Adoption KPI Tree extension

The Adoption KPI Tree provides a structure for separating:

* access
* activation
* workflow integration
* value creation
* quality stability
* risk management
* governance maturity
* scaling readiness

## 10. Enterprise knowledge-work AI pilots

## Why they are relevant

Many AI adoption cases begin as knowledge-work pilots.

These may include:

* content drafting
* knowledge retrieval
* research assistance
* summarization
* decision preparation
* internal communication
* customer support
* HR or learning support
* legal or compliance support

## Adoption theme

Knowledge-work augmentation and human-in-the-loop workflows.

## Signals to study

* first meaningful use
* repeat use
* quality after review
* source reliability
* human review points
* time saved
* expert attention shifted
* risk escalation
* trust by role
* governance questions

## Relevance for the Adoption KPI Tree

Knowledge-work AI pilots are especially useful because raw usage is often misleading.

A tool may be used frequently without improving the workflow or producing reliable outputs.

## Adoption KPI Tree extension

The Adoption KPI Tree helps separate:

* curiosity from adoption
* usage from value
* speed from quality
* confidence from proficiency
* experimentation from production use
* governance policy from operational governance
